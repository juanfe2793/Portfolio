The SRE title is a misnomer. We don't *engineer reliability* — reliability is the residual of every other decision the organization makes. What we actually do is hold the line when entropy wins.

{/* truncate */}

I've spent a decade doing that work: on-premise racks in Bogotá, then EKS clusters at Twilio handling 3M+ requests per second. The infrastructure changed; the fundamental tension didn't. Every high-scale system is a negotiation between the people who build features and the people who keep the lights on — and in most organizations, that negotiation is never written down.

That's what this log is for.

---

**What I'll cover here:**

- **Platform leadership** — the decisions behind the decisions. Why we built the DNS boundary the way we did. What the API Gateway architecture actually cost to operate, not just to build.
- **SRE philosophy** — reliability as a *property* of organizational behavior, not a team you hire.
- **AI in infrastructure** — I'm actively integrating Claude into my workflow at the infrastructure layer. The results have been surprising in both directions.
- **Operational runbooks** — principles distilled from incidents, written for engineers who inherit systems they didn't design.

The writing will be technical. I'll assume you know what a control plane is, what happens when you misconfigure a CoreDNS ConfigMap, and why `kubectl drain` sometimes isn't enough.

If that's your world — welcome to the log.
